Top Attractions

Highgrove Gardens

The private residence of HRH The Prince of Wales and Duchess of Cornwall. The gardens are beautifully landscaped with a variety of plantings.

Tip: Pre-book a tour in advance as access is limited.

Westonbirt Arboretum

One of the most important arboretums in the UK, featuring over 15,000 tree specimens. Highlights include the autumn colors and the Treetop Walkway.

Opening Hours: Open year-round, though times vary seasonally.

St Mary the Virgin and St Mary Magdalen Church

An impressive parish church dating back to the 13th century, known for its towering spire and beautiful interior.

Notable Features: The intricately carved wooden ceilings and stained glass windows.

Tetbury Police Museum & Courtroom

A small museum located in the old police station and courtroom, offering a glimpse into local law enforcement history.

Fun Fact: Includes displays of historic police uniforms and memorabilia.

Shopping

Antique Shops

Top Banana Antiques Mall and Lorfords Antiques offer extensive collections of antiques and collectibles.

Independent Boutiques

French Grey offers a charming selection of homeware and gifts, while The Highgrove Shop sells exclusive products inspired by the nearby Highgrove Gardens.

Specialty Stores

House of Cheese is a must-visit for cheese lovers, and Hobbs House Bakery is famous for its artisan bread and baked goods.

Dining

Cafes and Tearooms

Lyndseys and The Blue Zucchini Brasserie offer delightful options for breakfast and lunch.

Pubs and Restaurants

The Snooty Fox and The Ormond at Tetbury provide hearty meals and local ales in a warm ambiance.

Sweet Treats

The Sweet Pea Cafe & More is known for its delicious cakes, and The Priory Inn has an excellent dessert menu.

Leisure and Relaxation

Woolsack Day

If you visit in late May, don't miss Woolsack Day, a traditional local festival featuring races, live music, and food stalls.

Tetbury Trail

A self-guided walking tour available from the Tetbury Visitor Information Centre. Learn about the town’s history and notable buildings at your own pace.

Heritage Walk

Enjoy a leisurely walk through the town to appreciate the historic architecture and charming streetscapes. Look out for the blue plaques on buildings that highlight their historical significance.

Events and Markets

Weekly Markets

Held every Wednesday and Saturday in the Market House, offering a variety of local products. Arrive early for the best selection of fresh produce and handmade crafts.

Tetbury Music Festival

Held annually in the autumn, featuring classical music performances by renowned artists. Enjoy concerts in the beautiful setting of St Mary’s Church and other local venues.

Christmas in Tetbury

Experience the festive atmosphere with Christmas markets, lights, and events throughout December. The annual Christmas lights switch-on event is a local favorite.
